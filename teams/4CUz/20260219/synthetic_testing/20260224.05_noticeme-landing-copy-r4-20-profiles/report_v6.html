V6 Synthetic User Testing Report

⚠ Synthetic testing notice: This report uses synthetic personas generated and evaluated by AI (Claude, Sonnet 4.5) to simulate user reactions to landing page copy. These are not real users. Results reflect how an AI model interprets persona profiles and predicts their reactions, which is useful for directional signal and rapid iteration but is not a substitute for real user testing. Treat findings as hypotheses to validate, not conclusions.
Models: Claude Sonnet 4.5 (claude-sonnet-4-5-20250929) for persona evaluations. Claude Haiku 4.5 for clarity scoring and data compilation.

Governing Question

Would this person start a free trial after reading this landing page?

Method

20 synthetic personas evaluated the landing page copy. Each persona rated resonance, intent, and conversion confidence on a 5-point Likert scale (strongly disagree → strongly agree), plus qualitative feedback on what worked and what didn't. Personas are instructed to lean negative to counter AI sycophancy bias.

Executive Summary

This constrained run reached majority conversion-confidence by V6 with reduced dealbreaker rates.

Conversion Funnel

85% resonated with the copy (agree+). Of those, 75% expressed intent to try it, and 55% felt confident they'd convert.

Researcher's Stance

Additional run confirms improving conversion trajectory under the specified audience constraints.

— 🤖 Claude

Key Findings

  • Core framing held: reducing scroll without deletion remained persuasive.
  • Trust controls adoption: privacy and proof language continue to influence uptake.

Performance by Audience Segment

Privacy Guarded leads conversion at 100%. Deletion Rebounder has the lowest resonance at 75%.
A dealbreaker means the persona identified a reason they would not sign up regardless of other factors. Overall rate: 5%. Highest: Skeptical Optimizer at 25%.

Recommendations

  1. Keep mechanism explicit.
  2. Add stronger evidence and privacy defaults.

Qualitative Themes

  • Middle path beats bans.
  • Social accountability needs trust.

Price Perception & Clarity

Clarity improved round-over-round, with V6 skewing toward nailed_it.

# Copy V6

## Hero
Scroll less without deleting social media.

## Problem
You are not lazy. The feed is engineered to keep you there, and self-imposed blocks fail because you can always remove them.

## Mechanism
NoticeMe adds social accountability for attention: trusted friends see lightweight screen-time signals, creating enough pressure to help you exit loops.

## Privacy
You control who sees what. Share categories or top apps, not every detail.

## Proof
Interview pattern: students repeatedly delete apps then reinstall; social visibility is the only approach that felt sustainable.

## Pricing
Free tier + Premium at $5.99/month for deeper trend insights.

## CTA
Start your 7-day reset with your accountability circle.
