V6 Synthetic User Testing Report

⚠ Synthetic testing notice: This report uses synthetic personas generated and evaluated by AI (Claude, Sonnet 4.5) to simulate user reactions to landing page copy. These are not real users. Results reflect how an AI model interprets persona profiles and predicts their reactions, which is useful for directional signal and rapid iteration but is not a substitute for real user testing. Treat findings as hypotheses to validate, not conclusions.
Models: Claude Sonnet 4.5 (claude-sonnet-4-5-20250929) for persona evaluations. Claude Haiku 4.5 for clarity scoring and data compilation.

Governing Question

Would this person start a free trial after reading this landing page?

Method

60 synthetic personas evaluated the landing page copy. Each persona rated resonance, intent, and conversion confidence on a 5-point Likert scale (strongly disagree → strongly agree), plus qualitative feedback on what worked and what didn't. Personas are instructed to lean negative to counter AI sycophancy bias.

Audience Segments

  • Deletion Rebounder (12) — Uses app deletion as a temporary tactic and consistently returns to old patterns.
  • Privacy Guarded (12) — Interested in the outcome but evaluates data-sharing risk before trying anything.
  • Self Control Fatigued (12) — Aware of the issue and repeatedly attempts fixes, but slips under stress and exhaustion.
  • Skeptical Optimizer (12) — Needs mechanism transparency and evidence before adopting new tools.
  • Social Motivated (12) — Behavior changes most when trusted peers can see progress and provide light pressure.

Executive Summary

Second run with new synthetic audience data again crosses the majority conversion threshold by V6.

Conversion Funnel

70% resonated with the copy (agree+). Of those, 72% expressed intent to try it, and 62% felt confident they'd convert.

Researcher's Stance

R2 confirms the trajectory: clearer mechanism + explicit data controls increase confidence to try, while dealbreakers shrink meaningfully.

— 🤖 Claude

Key Findings

  • Mechanism trust improved: "not a blocker" framing remained critical.
  • Data-control messaging mattered: privacy-sensitive personas improved after explicit controls.
  • Proof requests persist: skeptical segments still ask for stronger evidence language.

Performance by Audience Segment

Self Control Fatigued leads conversion at 100%. Privacy Guarded has the lowest resonance at 50%.
A dealbreaker means the persona identified a reason they would not sign up regardless of other factors. Overall rate: 5%. Highest: Social Motivated at 17%.

Recommendations

  1. Add concrete proof tile: include pilot retention or reduction metric.
  2. Pre-CTA privacy checklist: who sees what, revocation, and defaults.
  3. Segmented onboarding: emphasize friend-circle setup early for social-motivated users.

Qualitative Themes

  • Middle path wins: users prefer reducing scroll without deleting apps.
  • Trust precedes adoption: controls and transparency unlock intent.
  • Social context is lever: accountability works best with trusted peers.

Price Perception & Clarity

V6 clarity is mostly nailed_it with a small residual partial group.

# Copy V6

## Hero
Scroll less without deleting social media.

## Problem
You are not lazy. The feed is engineered to keep you there, and self-imposed blocks fail because you can always remove them.

## Mechanism
NoticeMe adds social accountability for attention: trusted friends see lightweight screen-time signals, creating enough pressure to help you exit loops.

## Privacy
You control who sees what. Share categories or top apps, not every detail.

## Proof
Interview pattern: students repeatedly delete apps then reinstall; social visibility is the only approach that felt sustainable.

## Pricing
Free tier + Premium at $5.99/month for deeper trend insights.

## CTA
Start your 7-day reset with your accountability circle.
