⚠ Synthetic testing notice: This report uses synthetic personas generated and evaluated by AI (Claude, Sonnet 4.5) to simulate user reactions to landing page copy. These are not real users. Results reflect how an AI model interprets persona profiles and predicts their reactions, which is useful for directional signal and rapid iteration but is not a substitute for real user testing. Treat findings as hypotheses to validate, not conclusions.

Governing Question

Would this person start a free trial after reading this landing page?

Method

60 synthetic personas evaluated the landing page copy. Each persona rated resonance, intent, and conversion confidence on a 5-point Likert scale (strongly disagree → strongly agree), plus qualitative feedback on what worked and what didn't. Personas are instructed to lean negative to counter AI sycophancy bias.

Audience Segments

  • Deletion Rebounder (12) — Deletes social apps in bursts, then reinstalls when social need or boredom returns.
  • Privacy Guarded (12) — Interested in behavior change but cautious about sharing app-level usage data.
  • Self Control Fatigued (12) — Knows scrolling is harmful, repeatedly restarts, and is emotionally drained by failure cycles.
  • Skeptical Optimizer (12) — Demands mechanism clarity, evidence, and concrete value before adopting new tools.
  • Social Motivated (12) — Responds to peer comparison and accountability loops more than solo discipline tools.

Executive Summary

NoticeMe messaging improved consistently from baseline to V6, with stronger resonance and intent once copy clarified social accountability (not blocking) and explicit privacy controls.

Conversion Funnel

77% resonated with the copy (agree+). Of those, 55% expressed intent to try it, and 52% felt confident they'd convert. The biggest drop-off is from resonance to intent: people relate to the problem but aren't sold on the solution.

Researcher's Stance

V6 reaches majority conversion-confidence agreement while maintaining lower dealbreaker rates versus early rounds. The remaining risk is concentrated in privacy sensitivity and proof-seeking segments.

— 🤖 Claude

Key Findings

  • Core framing worked: "scroll less without deleting" significantly improved resonance.
  • Mechanism clarity mattered: explicit contrast vs blockers reduced skepticism.
  • Residual objections remain: privacy granularity and evidence depth are still requested.

Performance by Audience Segment

Privacy Guarded leads conversion at 83%. Deletion Rebounder has the lowest resonance at 58%.
A dealbreaker means the persona identified a reason they would not sign up regardless of other factors. Overall rate: 5%. Highest: Deletion Rebounder at 8%.

Recommendations

  1. Add privacy module near CTA: include friend-level visibility examples.
  2. Add lightweight proof section: include pilot metrics and retention signal.
  3. Keep tone playful-not-punitive: avoid shame-heavy language in hero and proof.

Qualitative Themes

  • Realism over willpower: users trust social nudges more than hard blockers.
  • Trust controls adoption: privacy clarity strongly influences willingness to try.
  • Peer context is leverage: accountability value rises with trusted-friend groups.

Price Perception & Clarity

Clarity scoring improved through each round; V6 shows a strong nailed_it majority.

# Copy V6

## Hero
Scroll less without deleting social media.

## Problem
You are not lazy. The feed is engineered to keep you there, and self-imposed blocks fail because you can always remove them.

## Mechanism
NoticeMe adds social accountability for attention: trusted friends see lightweight screen-time signals, creating enough pressure to help you exit loops.

## Privacy
You control who sees what. Share categories or top apps, not every detail.

## Proof
Interview pattern: students repeatedly delete apps then reinstall; social visibility is the only approach that felt sustainable.

## Pricing
Free tier + Premium at $5.99/month for deeper trend insights.

## CTA
Start your 7-day attention reset.
